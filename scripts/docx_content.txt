[P20][None] Why This Book
[P22][None] Agriculture runs on numbers. From plant population and seed rate to feed conversion ratio and genetic gain, clear formulas turn field observations into decisions. This handbook assembles the core calculations, indices, and decision rules used in crop and animal production, and wraps them with worked examples, assumptions, and pitfalls so you can apply them confidently in research, extension, and farm management.
[P23][None] Unlike a bare list of equations, each metric here is placed in context: what it measures, when to use it, what inputs you need, how sensitive it is to measurement error, and what to do next once you've computed it.

[P26][None] • Researchers & graduate students who need defensible, citable calculations and experimental design quickrefs.
[P27][None] • Extension professionals & agribusiness advisors who make rapid recommendations under uncertainty.
[P28][None] • Farm managers & technicians who convert measurements into actions.
[P29][None] • Policy and development practitioners who compare systems using harmonized sustainability and economics metrics.

[P43][Heading 1] Chapter 1  Units, Conversions & Calculation Hygiene
[P45][None] Most calculation errors in agronomy and animal production are not due to the formulas themselves but to unit mismatches, rounding errors, or using industry vs. SI conventions inconsistently.
[P46][Heading 2] 1.1 Core Unit Systems
[P47][None] SI Units (default in this book): Length: meter (m) · Area: ha (10,000 m²) · Volume: liter (L) · Mass: kg · Temperature: °C · Energy: MJ
[P48][None] Agricultural practice units (still common): Acre (0.405 ha) · Inch of water · Cusec (ft³/s) · Bushel · lb, gal, cwt in livestock.
[P49][None] ⚠  Pitfall: Many indices mix SI and non-SI in extension bulletins. Always normalize to SI, then convert back for reporting if needed.
[P51][Heading 2] 1.2 Essential Conversion Factors
[P52][None] Quick Reference
[P54][Heading 2] 1.3 Calculation Hygiene Rules
[P55][None] 1. Write the equation with units before inserting numbers.
[P56][None] 2. Convert all terms to SI; if final answer needed in local units, convert last.
[P57][None] 3. Keep significant figures until final step; then round.
[P58][None] 4. Check plausibility against typical ranges.
[P59][None] 5. Document sources: field measurement, lab test, or extension reference.

2.1  Plant Population (PP) | 2.1  Plant Population (PP)
PP = (Area m² × 10,000) / (Row Spacing cm × Plant Spacing cm) | PP = (Area m² × 10,000) / (Row Spacing cm × Plant Spacing cm)
Variables
Area = m²
Spacing = cm
Output = plants/ha | Purpose
Estimate plants per hectare based on chosen spacing.
Example
Maize at 75 cm × 25 cm: PP = 10,000 / (0.75 × 0.25) = 53,333 plants/ha.
⚠ Pitfall: Confusing cm and m; forgetting field mortality.

2.2  Seed Rate (SR) | 2.2  Seed Rate (SR)
SR = (Target Population × Test Weight g) / (1000 × 100 × Germination% × Purity%) | SR = (Target Population × Test Weight g) / (1000 × 100 × Germination% × Purity%)
Variables
Test Weight = g per 1000 seeds
Germination & Purity = decimal (0.90 not 90) | Purpose
Translate target population to kg seed to purchase.
Example
350,000 plants/ha, 35 g TW, 90% germ, 98% purity → SR = 126 kg/ha.
⚠ Pitfall: Misplacing % as whole number instead of decimal.

Variables
Purity = % pure seed by weight
Germination = % viable | Purpose
Proportion of seed lot that is viable and pure.
Example
Purity 98%, germination 90% → RVS = 88.2%.

3.1  Cropping Intensity (CI) | 3.1  Cropping Intensity (CI)
CI (%) = Gross Cropped Area / Net Cultivable Area × 100 | CI (%) = Gross Cropped Area / Net Cultivable Area × 100
Variables
Gross Cropped Area = total area sown across all seasons | Purpose
Measures how many times land is cropped in a year.

--- Table 10 ---
3.9  Land Equivalent Ratio (LER) ★ Key Index | 3.9  Land Equivalent Ratio (LER) ★ Key Index
LER = Yia/Ysa + Yib/Ysb | LER = Yia/Ysa + Yib/Ysb
Variables
Yia, Yib = intercrop yields of species a and b
Ysa, Ysb = sole crop yields | Purpose
Measures yield advantage of intercropping vs. monoculture.
Example
Maize/cowpea intercrop with LER = 1.35 → 35% land advantage.
⚠ Pitfall: Always compare to same season sole crop controls.

--- Table 11 ---
3.14  Monetary Advantage Index (MAI) | 3.14  Monetary Advantage Index (MAI)
MAI = ((LER-1) / LER) × Total Monetary Value | MAI = ((LER-1) / LER) × Total Monetary Value
Variables
LER = Land Equivalent Ratio
TMV = monetary value of total intercrop produce | Purpose
Captures the financial advantage of intercropping over monoculture.
Example
LER = 1.35, TMV = $2000/ha → MAI = $518/ha.
